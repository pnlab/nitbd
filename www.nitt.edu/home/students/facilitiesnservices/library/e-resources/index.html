    e-Resource Access and Copyright Rules

    Remote Access to E-Resources through INFED: Click here

    Access E-Resources from remote through INFED/Shibboleth. You have to login with your OCTA username (Usually your roll number/ webmail username without "@nitt.edu") and password. Remember, OCTA password is not the same as your webmail password. If you have forgotten your OCTA password, please contact the Computer Support Group (CSG) over email (csgmaint@nitt.edu)

    Remote Access to E-Resources through MyLOFT

    Central Library is providing remote access to all subscribed e-resources for Faculties, PDFs and PhD research scholars through MyLOFT Application. PhD research scholars need to send a request email to librarian@nitt.edu for their account creation. After your account is created, a welcome mail reaches your institute affiliated email id (if not found, check Spam folder/ MyLOFT in Junk Summary). There is an option to “Set Password” in that mail. Click on the option. It will open a new tab and will ask you to create a password.

    Open MyLOFT app in your desktop: Click here

    While using MyLOFT on desktop, remember to download the browser extension. Chrome Users   Microsoft Edge Users

    Downlaod MyLOFT for your Mobile. Android users   Mac users

    1. IEEE/IET Electronic Library (IEL): Click here

      IEEE/IET Electronic Library (IEL) covers almost one-third of the world's current electrical engineering and computer science literature, providing unparalleled access to publications from the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) and the IET. The resource covers more than 780,000 documents from over 12,000 publications, including 120 journals, transactions, magazines, conference proceedings, IEEE Standards. More than 25,000 new pages are added per month. It provides access to more than two million full-page PDF images, including all original charts, graphs, diagrams, photographs, and illustrative material.

    2. Elsevier's Science Direct: Click here

      ScienceDirect is the web-based interface to the full-text database of Elsevier Science journals and Academic Press (Ideal), one of the world's largest providers of scientific, technical and medical (STM) literature. The ScienceDirect offers a rich electronic environment for research journals, bibliographic databases and reference works. The database offers more than 1700 scientific, technical and medical peer-reviewed journals, over 59 million abstracts, over two million full-text scientific journal articles, an expanding suite of bibliographic databases and linking to another one million full-text articles via CrossRef to other publishers' platforms.

      List of Journal Titles Accessible from Elsevier ScienceDirect

    3. Springer Link 1700 Collection and Nature Journal: Click here

      The Springer's Link is the e-journals service from Springer, one of the world's leading scientific publishers. Key subject areas include: Mathematics, Computer Science, Physics, Astronomy, Geoscience, Chemistry, Engineering and Medicine. The resource include over 1700 current journals of the highest quality.

      List of Journal Titles Accessible from Springer

    4. American Chemical Society's Journals(ACS)-All Web Edition: http://pubs.acs.org/journal/

      ACS Publications manages the scholarly publishing program of the world’s largest and most influential scientific society. Every year over 100,000 authors and their research teams from the community of scientists worldwide submit their work for consideration, review, and publication to ACS Publications. You can access articles published from ACS journals after 1996.

    5. SciFinder-N: Click here

      SciFinder gives you the access to CAS content that includes many scientific disciplines including biological sciences, biomedical sciences, engineering, materials science, agricultural science and more.

      This platform provides access to journals, books, and databases from RSC Publishing, linking over 1,162,083 chemical science articles and chapters. You can access the latest research of interest using the custom eAlerts, RSS feeds and blogs or you can explore content using the quick and advanced searches. Discover the highest quality integrated scientific research today - search faster, navigate smarter and connect more.

      Bentham Science currently publishes more than 100 journals. These journals cover various disciplines in pharmaceutical research and development, medical subspecialties, engineering, technology, and social sciences.

    13. EBSCO Business Source Elite: Click here

      Business Source Elite database provides full-text coverage of top business, management and economics journals and periodicals. It covers topics such as accounting, banking, finance, international business, marketing, and sales.

    14. CMIE-Prowess IQ: Click here

    15. Proquest Science (Formerly ASTP): http://www.proquest.com/

      The Applied Science and Technology Plus (ASTP) is a CD-ROM database (with access to the Web). The database provides indices and full abstracts to more than 556 key science and engineering titles, plus a full-image of 160 titles. All titles are indexed from 1994 onward; the database is updated monthly.

      1. ABI/Inform Collection: Click here

        The ABI/Inform Collection is one of the world's first electronic databases. It has been a premier source of business information for more than 30 years. The database contains content from thousands of journals that help researchers track business conditions, trends, management techniques, corporate strategies, and industry-specific topics worldwide. It consists of 1800 full-text journals and 2000 journals that are indexed and abstracted. The resource is offered on Web with CD ROM backup. Back-files are available from 1985-Present.

    17. Project Muse: Click Here

      Project MUSE provides full-text access scholarly journals in the Humanities and Social sciences. The database is a collaboration between Johns Hopkins University and other university presses and not-for-profit publishers. This collection covers Asian and Pacific Studies, Poetry, Fiction and Creative Non-Fiction.

    18. South Asia Archive (SAA): Click here

      It is an extensive resource for students and scholars across the humanities and social sciences. The historical documents within the Archive are truly interdisciplinary, reflecting the varied range of knowledge production in colonial and early post-colonial India and the wider sub-continent.

    20. ACM Digital Library: http://dl.acm.org

      The ACM Digital Library incorporates digital versions of works published by ACM since its inception. The major components of the resource is an enhanced version of the ACM Digital Library plus an extended bibliographic database, consisting of more than a quarter-million citations of core works in computing. The ACM Digital Library hosts over 103,000 full-text articles from ACM journals, magazines, and conference proceedings and half million bibliographic Records with about 2,50,000 links to full bibliographic information and 70,000 further links to full-text resources.

    24. ASCE Journals: Click here

      The American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE) is recognized globally for their significant contribution and dedication to the advancement of science and education in the civil engineering profession. The ASCE publishes 30 journals, periodicals and transactions that cover a comprehensive range of the civil engineering profession. ASCE journals are highly cited and are most relevant to the civil engineers for exchanging technical and professional knowledge. Information published in the journals of ASCE forms archival records not only of the technical advances of the ASCE but of the civil engineering profession as a whole.

    25. ASME Journals: http://asmedigitalcollection.asme.org

      The American Society of Mechanical Engineers is a nonprofit educational and technical organization serving a worldwide community of mechanical engineers. The ASME conducts one of the world's largest technical publishing operations. The society holds more than 30 technical conferences and 200 professional development courses each year. The ASME promote and enhance the technical competency and professional well-being through quality programs and activities in mechanical engineering, better enable its practitioners to contribute to the well-being of humankind through its publications that include more than 20 journals and transactions.

    26. Economic & Political Weekly: Click here

      A weekly peer-reviewed academic journal covering all social sciences. The journal was established in 1949 as the Economic Weekly. It is among the most prestigious scholarly journals in India.

    27. Institute for Studies in Industrial Development (ISID) Database: Click here

      ISID has been acquiring databases and updating the existing databases to meet the data requirements of the empirical researchers in the area of macroeconomic aspects of the Indian economy. In particular, the ISID database covers a wide range of aggregate economic data with special emphasis on Indian manufacturing industries, corporate sectors, foreign direct investments, technology, international trade, labor, and social sector. The coverage and content of these databases are being continuously enlarged and updated

    28. SIAM Journals: Click here

      Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics is a nonprofit organization to convey useful mathematical knowledge to other professionals who could implement mathematical theory for practical, industrial, or scientific use. Through publications, research, and community SIAM builds cooperation between mathematics and the worlds of science and technology. SIAM publishes 17 peer-reviewed journals.

    29. MathSciNet: Click here

      It offers access to a carefully maintained and easily searchable database of reviews, abstracts and bibliographic information for much of the mathematical sciences literature. Over 125,000 new items are added each year, most of them classified according to the Mathematics Subject Classification.

    30. ASTM Standards: Click here

      The ASTM Standards and Engineering Digital Library is a vast collection of industry-leading standards and technical engineering information. The Library covers a broad range of engineering disciplines, including aerospace, biomedical, chemical, civil, environmental, geological, health and safety, industrial, materials science, mechanical, nuclear, petroleum, soil science, and solar engineering.

    33. Web of Science: Click here

      Web of Science (previously known as Web of Knowledge) is an online scientific citation indexing service originally produced by the Institute for Scientific Information (ISI). It provides a comprehensive citation search.

    34. SCOPUS: Click here

      Scopus is the largest abstract and citation database of peer-reviewed literature: scientific journals, books and conference proceedings. Delivering a comprehensive overview of the world's research output in the fields of science, technology, medicine, social sciences, and arts and humanities, Scopus features smart tools to track, analyze and visualize research.

    35. SciVal: Click here

      SciVal offers quick, easy access to visualize research performance, benchmark relative to peers, develop collaborative partnerships and analyze research trends. It is based on output and usage data (from 1996 to the current date, covers over 48 million records) from Scopus. Create your account in SciVal with your NITT email ID and sign in.

    39. Turnitin Similarity Checking Tool: http://turnitin.com

      Turnitin is an Internet-based plagiarism detection service. You can generate similarity report of your research article, assignment and project reports. Research scholars and students may approach to their respective guide to have the research scholar/ student account.

    40. Ouriginal Similarity Checking Tool: Click here

      Ouriginal is globally recognized as plagiarism detection tool. Faculty members and research scholars are using this tool for creation of original Academic Content. To have an Ouriginal account, send an email request to librarian@nitt.edu. Before submitting any article/ research paper to Ouriginal go through the instructions and submission guideline carefully.

    41. Grammarly Writing Support Tool: https://www.grammarly.com/edu

      Grammarly is an application tool for checking spelling, and grammar for research writing. It automatically detects potential issues in the text/ document and suggests context-specific corrections for grammar, spelling, punctuation, word choice, style, and plagiarism. To get the all features users need to sign up with their institutional email ID.

      Shodhgangotri is a repository of electronic version of synopses/research proposals submitted to universities for registering in PhD.

    49. Networked Digital Library of Theses and Dissertations (NDLTD): Click here

      NDLTD promotes the adoption, creation, use, dissemination, and preservation of electronic theses and dissertations (ETDs). Research scholars can search freely-available digitized dissertations from around the world.

    50. BASE (Bielefeld Academic Search Engine): Click here

      BASE is one of the world's most voluminous search engine especially for academic web resources. Users can access full texts of about 60% of the indexed documents (books, articles, thesis, patents, maps, reports, course materails, musical notations, images, audios, videos, software, and dataset etc.) for free (Open Access) from more than 150 million documents from 7,000 institutional repositories, and digital collections.

    51. CORE (COnnecting REpositories): Click here

      CORE is an aggregator of open access papers/ articles. Users can search and download from CORE's discovery service and can get recommendations of related article/ document covering their research interest. CORE's repository dashboard facilitates users' content management and access to raw dataset.

    52. RePEc (Research Papers in Economics): Click here

      RePEc is a collaborative effort to enhance the dissemination of research in Economics and related sciences. More precisely, users can search in a decentralized bibliographic database for working papers, journal articles, books, books chapters and software components.

    53. JURN: Click here

      JURN is a search tool to find free academic articles and books through cureted index. More precisely, users can search in a decentralized cureted index of open access journal articles, books, books chapters covering Arts and Humanities, Bio-medical, Business, Law and Science fields.

    54. Digital Common Network: Click here

      Digital Commons Network provides free access to full-text scholarly articles and other research from hundreds of universities and colleges worldwide. Curated by university librarians and their supporting institutions, this dynamic research tool includes peer-reviewed journal articles, book chapters, dissertations, working papers, conference proceedings, and other original scholarly work.
